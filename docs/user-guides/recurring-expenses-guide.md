# Recurring Expenses User Guide

## What are Recurring Expenses?

Recurring expenses are payments that happen automatically on a regular schedule. Think of rent, subscriptions, utilities, gym memberships, or any other expense you pay repeatedly. Instead of manually creating the same expense every month, FairPay can automate this for you.

## Getting Started

### Creating Your First Recurring Expense

1. **Navigate to Recurring Expenses**
   - Click "Recurring" in the bottom navigation (mobile)
   - Or click "Recurring Expenses" in the sidebar (desktop)

2. **Click "Create Recurring"**
   - Desktop: Click the button in the page header
   - Mobile: Tap the "+" icon in the top-right corner
   - Keyboard: Press `N` key

3. **Select Context**
   - Choose the group or friend this expense belongs to
   - This determines who shares the expense

4. **Fill Out Expense Details**
   - **Description**: What is this expense? (e.g., "Netflix Subscription", "Rent", "Gym Membership")
   - **Amount**: How much does it cost?
   - **Category**: Select a category (optional but helpful for analytics)
   - **Paid By**: Who pays this expense initially?
   - **Split Method**: How should it be split among participants?

5. **Configure Recurring Schedule**
   - **Frequency**: How often does it repeat?
     - Weekly (every 7 days)
     - Bi-weekly (every 14 days)
     - Monthly (once a month)
     - Quarterly (every 3 months)
     - Yearly (once a year)
   - **Repeat Every**: Adjust if you need custom intervals (e.g., every 2 months)
   - **End Date** (Optional): When should this stop recurring?
     - Leave blank for ongoing expenses
     - Set a date for finite expenses (e.g., 12-month gym contract)

6. **Save**
   - Review your settings
   - Click "Create" to save

## Managing Recurring Expenses

### Viewing Your Expenses

**Three Tabs:**
- **Active Tab** (Press `1`): Shows all running recurring expenses
- **Paused Tab** (Press `2`): Shows temporarily disabled expenses
- **Analytics Tab** (Press `3`): Shows spending insights and trends

**Dashboard Stats:**
- **Total**: Count of all recurring expenses
- **Active**: Count of currently active expenses
- **Paused**: Count of paused expenses
- **Monthly Total**: Sum of all active expenses normalized to monthly cost

**Upcoming Preview:**
- See which expenses will be created in the next 7 days
- Helps you plan ahead for upcoming payments

### Editing a Recurring Expense

1. Click the three-dot menu (⋮) on any expense card
2. Select "Edit"
3. Modify:
   - Frequency (change from monthly to weekly, etc.)
   - Interval (change from every 1 month to every 2 months)
   - End date (add or remove an end date)
4. Click "Save"

**Note:** You cannot edit the expense amount or description through the recurring edit dialog. To change those, you'll need to delete and recreate the recurring expense.

### Pausing a Recurring Expense

**Why Pause?**
- Temporarily stop expense creation without deleting
- Useful for seasonal expenses (e.g., summer pool membership)
- Keep the history and settings intact

**How to Pause:**
- **Desktop**: Click three-dot menu → Pause
- **Mobile**: Swipe right on the card → Tap "Pause"

**What Happens:**
- Future expenses won't be created automatically
- Past expenses remain unchanged
- You can resume anytime

### Resuming a Paused Expense

1. Go to the "Paused" tab
2. Find the expense you want to resume
3. **Desktop**: Click three-dot menu → Resume
4. **Mobile**: Swipe right → Tap "Resume"

**What Happens:**
- The expense becomes active again
- Next occurrence is recalculated from today
- Automatic expense creation resumes

### Skipping the Next Occurrence

Sometimes you need to skip just one payment:

1. Click three-dot menu on the expense
2. Select "Skip Next"
3. The next scheduled expense is skipped
4. The following occurrence becomes the new "next"

**Example:**
- You have a monthly subscription due on the 15th
- You skip the next occurrence on January 5th
- January 15th expense is skipped
- Next expense will be February 15th

### Deleting a Recurring Expense

**Warning:** This cannot be undone!

1. Click three-dot menu on the expense
2. Select "Delete"
3. Read the confirmation message carefully
4. Click "Delete" to confirm

**What Happens:**
- The recurring schedule is permanently deleted
- Future expenses won't be created
- Past expenses created from this template remain unchanged
- You cannot recover the recurring settings

## Mobile Features

### Swipe Actions

On mobile, you can swipe right on any expense card to reveal quick actions:

- **Swipe Right → First Button**: Pause or Resume (depending on current state)
- **Swipe Right → Second Button**: Delete (red button)

**Tips:**
- Swipe at least 40% across the card to trigger the action
- Swipe actions only work on touch screens
- Desktop users use the three-dot menu instead

### Bottom Sheet Dialogs

On mobile, create and edit dialogs appear as bottom sheets:
- Slides up from the bottom of the screen
- Drag handle at the top for easy dismissal
- Full-width buttons for easier tapping
- Optimized for one-handed use

## Notifications

### Notification Center

Access via the bell icon in the header:

**Overdue Expenses:**
- Shows expenses that should have been created but weren't
- Marked in red for visibility
- Click to go to recurring expenses page

**Upcoming This Week:**
- Shows expenses that will be created in the next 7 days
- Helps you prepare for upcoming payments
- Click to view full details

**Badge Counter:**
- Shows total number of notifications (overdue + upcoming)
- Updates automatically
- Maximum displays as "9+"

### Toast Notifications

You'll see toast notifications (small popups) when you:
- Pause an expense: "Recurring expense paused"
- Resume an expense: "Recurring expense resumed"
- Skip an occurrence: "Next occurrence skipped"
- Delete an expense: "Recurring expense deleted"
- Encounter an error: Describes what went wrong

## Analytics & Insights

### Monthly Overview Card

**Monthly Total:**
- Sum of all active recurring expenses
- Normalized to monthly cost (even if paid quarterly or yearly)
- Helps you understand your monthly commitment

**Yearly Projection:**
- Monthly total × 12
- Shows annual impact of all recurring expenses

**Active/Paused Ratio:**
- Visual progress bar showing distribution
- Percentage of active vs total recurring expenses

### Category Breakdown Card

**Top 5 Categories:**
- See which categories cost the most
- Each category shows:
  - Number of expenses in that category
  - Total monthly cost
  - Percentage of overall spending

**Use Cases:**
- Identify where your money goes
- Find opportunities to reduce spending
- Track subscription creep

### Frequency Distribution Card

**Visual Distribution:**
- See how your expenses are distributed by frequency
- Shows count and percentage for each frequency type

**Insights:**
- Understand your payment schedule
- Identify if too many payments happen at once
- Plan cash flow better

### Quick Stats Card

**Four Key Metrics:**
1. **Total Expenses**: Count of all recurring expenses
2. **Average Monthly Cost**: Average cost per expense
3. **Categories**: Number of unique categories
4. **Active Rate**: Percentage that are active

## Best Practices

### Organizing Recurring Expenses

1. **Use Clear Descriptions**
   - ✅ "Netflix Premium Subscription"
   - ❌ "Netflix"
   - Helps you identify expenses quickly

2. **Set Accurate End Dates**
   - For contracts with known end dates
   - Prevents unnecessary expenses after contract ends
   - Examples: Gym contracts, limited-time subscriptions

3. **Categorize Everything**
   - Makes analytics more useful
   - Easier to find expenses
   - Better insights into spending patterns

4. **Review Regularly**
   - Check the Analytics tab monthly
   - Identify subscriptions you no longer use
   - Pause seasonal expenses during off-season

### When to Use Recurring vs Regular Expenses

**Use Recurring For:**
- ✅ Rent/Mortgage
- ✅ Subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, etc.)
- ✅ Utilities (if predictable amount)
- ✅ Gym memberships
- ✅ Insurance payments
- ✅ Any expense that repeats regularly

**Use Regular Expenses For:**
- ✅ One-time purchases
- ✅ Variable-amount expenses (groceries, dining out)
- ✅ Irregular expenses
- ✅ Expenses that don't follow a pattern

### Managing Seasonal Expenses

**Example: Pool Membership (May-September)**

Option 1: Set End Date
- Create in May
- Set end date for September 30
- Automatically stops after summer

Option 2: Pause/Resume
- Create without end date
- Pause in October
- Resume next May
- Keeps all settings intact

## Troubleshooting

### My recurring expense isn't showing up

**Check:**
1. Is it in the "Paused" tab?
2. Has the end date passed?
3. Is your filter set correctly?
4. Try refreshing the page

### The monthly total seems wrong

**Explanation:**
- Different frequencies are normalized to monthly cost
- A $120 yearly subscription shows as $10/month
- A $100 weekly payment shows as ~$433/month (4.33 weeks per month)

**Verify:**
- Check individual expense amounts
- Verify frequency settings are correct
- Use Analytics tab to see the breakdown

### Swipe actions aren't working

**Check:**
- Swipe actions only work on mobile/touch devices
- Use the three-dot menu on desktop instead
- Make sure you're swiping on the card itself, not on a button

### Notification badge is stuck

**Fix:**
1. Open the notification center
2. Click on each notification to mark as seen
3. The badge should clear automatically
4. If persists, refresh the page
